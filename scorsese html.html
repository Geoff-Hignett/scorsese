Martin Scorsese

The most nominated living director

A veteran of over five decades

Scorsese is an American director, producer, screenwriter, actor and film historian, who has been in the industry for more than 50 years. Mean Streets (1973) was his breakthrough movie depicting his experiences of life in New York City. Since then he has gone on to direct movies garnering huge levels of success including Taxi Driver (1976), Raging Bull (1980), The Color of Money (1986), Goodfellas (1990), The Age of Innocence (1993), Casino (1995), The Aviator (2004), The Departed (2006), Hugo (2011), and The Wolf of Wall Street (2013). His collection of work addresses such themes as Sicilian-American identity, Roman Catholic concepts of guilt and redemption, religion, organized crime, and gang conflict.

Widespread recognition

At eight Best Director nominations, he is the most nominated living director and shares the title of second-most nominations overall.
As a director he has won:

  • an Academy Award
  • a Palme d'Or
  • a Cannes Film Festival Best Director Award
  • a Silver Lion
  • a Grammy
  • Emmys
  • Golden Globes
  • BAFTAs
